\begin{abstract}
We introduce CSM-SAM, a cross-session memory extension of SAM2 for
longitudinal and bitemporal segmentation. A frozen SAM2 ViT-H encoder and
mask decoder are coupled with a small (\textasciitilde2M parameter)
cross-session memory attention module and an auxiliary change-map head
supervised by the XOR of pre- and mid-session masks, together with a
continuous weeks-elapsed embedding. We evaluate on HNTS-MRG 2024 (Task 2
mid-RT head and neck tumor segmentation), BraTS-GLI 2024 post-treatment
glioma, and four remote-sensing / disaster change-detection benchmarks.
Our goal is a single cross-session memory mechanism that transfers across
domains without retraining the backbone.
\end{abstract}
