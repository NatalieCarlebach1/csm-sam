\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Medical longitudinal segmentation is dominated by full-training 3D
specialists such as nnU-Net~\cite{isensee2021nnunet}, Swin
UNETR~\cite{hatamizadeh2021swinunetr}, UNETR~\cite{hatamizadeh2022unetr},
and MedNeXt~\cite{roy2023mednext}, typically ignoring the prior session.
Foundation-model segmentors --- SAM~\cite{kirillov2023sam},
SAM2~\cite{ravi2024sam2}, MedSAM2~\cite{ma2024medsam2},
DINOv2~\cite{oquab2024dinov2}, CLIPSeg~\cite{lueddecke2022clipseg},
TotalSegmentator~\cite{wasserthal2023totalsegmentator} --- offer strong
priors but do not model cross-session structure. On the remote-sensing
side, bitemporal change detectors (FC-Siam~\cite{daudt2018fcsiam},
SNUNet~\cite{fang2022snunet}, BIT~\cite{chen2021bit},
ChangeFormer~\cite{bandara2022changeformer},
Bi-SRNet~\cite{ding2022bisrnet}, SCanNet~\cite{ding2024scannet})
fuse via siamese concat / difference / transformer tokens on small
encoders. CSM-SAM unifies these lines by plugging a cross-session
attention module on top of a frozen SAM2 encoder.
