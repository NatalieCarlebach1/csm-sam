\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Adaptive radiotherapy, longitudinal disease monitoring, and remote-sensing
change detection all share a structural prior: a prior-session observation
of the same scene or patient is available at inference time. Existing
segmentation pipelines either discard this prior, concatenate it as an
input channel, or fuse it with shallow siamese features. We argue that a
more direct approach is to treat the prior session as a \emph{memory bank}
of tokens attended to by a frozen foundation backbone. CSM-SAM instantiates
this idea on top of SAM2~\cite{ravi2024sam2} and evaluates it on
medical longitudinal and remote-sensing bitemporal benchmarks.
